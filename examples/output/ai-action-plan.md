# AI Action Plan

For Jen Weigel — Program manager and chief-of-staff style operator for AI enablement

A prioritized guide to getting started with AI based on your workflow design session. Start at the top — these are your highest-leverage, lowest-risk starting points.

## Start Here (This Week)

These are Bucket 1 tasks — AI can handle them end-to-end. Try one prompt from your prompt pack and see how it goes.

- **Task and accountability operations** — Strong candidate for end-to-end AI with a light approval gate.
- **Agentic Audience standup** — Strong candidate for end-to-end AI with a light approval gate.

## Build Toward (This Month)

These are Bucket 2 tasks — AI + You. Use AI to reduce prep time, draft starting points, and surface patterns. You still own the final call.

- **KR 3.1 portfolio integration** — AI reduces prep, aggregation, and coordination load. You own synthesis and decisions.
- **Daily ops and triage** — AI reduces prep, aggregation, and coordination load. You own synthesis and decisions.

## Keep Human (Always)

These stay with you. AI can help you prepare, but the real work is judgment, relationships, and taste.

- **Monthly AI transformation narrative** — Keep human-led. Use AI for note cleanup or targeted research only.
- **Cohort-based AI acceleration design** — Keep human-led. Use AI for note cleanup or targeted research only.

## Going Further

Once you've tested the prompts and built confidence with Buckets 1 and 2:

- **Refine your prompts.** Save the ones that work. Edit the ones that don't. Build a personal prompt library.
- **Set up custom instructions.** Use the Custom AI Instructions output to personalize your AI tools so they know your context.
- **Consider agents.** If you have multiple Bucket 1 and 2 tasks that share inputs or cadences, the Agent Opportunity Map shows how they could cluster into dedicated AI agents with defined roles.
- **Revisit quarterly.** Your work changes. Re-run this interview when your responsibilities shift, and your prompts and leverage map will update with it.
- **Set up your second brain.** Use the Second Brain Setup Guide to create a personal knowledge repo or folder that captures decisions, meeting takeaways, and process notes so nothing lives only in your head.
